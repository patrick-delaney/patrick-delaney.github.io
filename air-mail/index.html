Air Mail News

Digital brand

UX/UI

IA

Helping a cheeky newsletter soar to new heights... and getting showcased in the 2021 Apple Keynote.

Client

Air Mail is digital newsletter helmed by former Vanity Fair editor-in-chief Graydon Carter and former New York Times reporter Alessandra Stanley.

Conceived as a weekly dispatch infused with breezy, sardonic cosmopolitanism, Air Mail's email-only format was reaching its limits.

Challenge

Air Mail needed to go above and beyond to reach new readers without touching the site's existing (and charming!) newsletter format.

Solution

Working with the client’s dev, design, and editorial teams, we crafted a comprehensive UX/UI/IA package that transformed a weekly missive into a daily destination without diluting Air Mail's passion for old school print publishing.

Goals

  • Create a seamless user journey to, from, and between Air Mail issues.
  • Surface evergreen content without requiring lots of curation
  • Increase readership, subscriptions, and ad clickthrough.

Not an award but whatever

The result: a brand new site and a surprise feature in Apple's 2021 Keynote! I guess Tim Cook is into gossip about British royalty?

Mail's here!

Air Mail’s format was a weekly email, issue page, and past issues list. No homepage, no search, no archive. The content deserved more!

The new, rebranded Air Mail added a homepage, navigation, and archive IA, creating a fresh experience still grounded in the weekly issue cadence.

Content strategy

It doesn't matter how glamorous you look, if you don't get your story straight, no one will listen. Like all good publications, we started with the content and built up from there.

Original UX

Simple, but restrictive. Users could find the latest issue but had to hunt and peck to find more content.

Revised UX

Adding a homepage and archive network provided multiple points of entry without breaking the site format or SEO.

Navigation

We introduced a holistic navigation system that promoted organic user journeys to, from and within newsletter issues.

Homepage nav + Search

Persistent issue toc

We also built a dynamic homepage hero that kept the upcoming Sunday issue above the cut. We treated the weekly format as a key AirMail strength, building anticipation and providing structure to content.

Back catalog

For dedicated archivists, we created category and author pages that received as much attention as the rest of the site. It was important to us that deep-diving felt just as encouraged as browsing the homepage or opening a new dispatch.

Brand

It took weeks of collaboration and revision to strike the proper balance of legacy print and contemporary tech.

Handoff

We worked closely with the engineering team to make sure Air Mail was future proof against unexpected turbulence.
