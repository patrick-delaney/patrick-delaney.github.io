brand + systems + content

2021 Apple Keynote

Time

12 months

Team

1 Product manager

Role

Design system, content strategy, digital branding

Air Mail is a digital magazine dealing in high society delivered in weekly email dispatches.

Helmed by Vanity Fair’s Graydon Carter and the New York Time’s Alessandra Stanley, Air Mail’s breezy, sardonic style had earned them a dedicated readership in less than a year.

Problem

Air Mail's email-only format was reaching its limit. How could Air Mail preserve it’s charm while improving recirc, ad-rev, and SEO traffic?

The Old Site

Email-only. No homepage, no search, each issue separated with a simple backlog archive that siloed content like a stack of old magazines.

Email + Archive

Solution

The fix was straightforward: build a website around the weekly issue that focused on SEO, ad-rev, time-on-site, and subs.

The New Site

We introduced a robust new information architecture without touching the core email / magazine product.

So how did we do it? Under the hood, our new system came together to promote a quality reading experience that also hammered home Air Mail’s core KPIs.

Content Strategy

We wanted users consuming content in the most intuitive way possible before we asked them to subscribe.

Old UX

Email only. Simple, but restrictive. Users could find the latest issue but had to hunt and peck to find more.

New UX

The new homepage / issue / archive IA provided multiple points of entry and user journeys without breaking the site format or SEO.

Dynamic Hero

We added a homepage hero that previewed next Sunday’s issue. We treated the weekly format as key to generating anticipation and structure.
