Project goals

Create a seamless user journey to, from, and between issues, surface evergreen content without lots of curation, and increase readership, subscriptions, and ad clickthrough.

Team

Role

Helmed by Vanity Fair's ex-editor-in-chief Graydon Carter

Air Mail was a passion project that started out as a breezy, sardonic newsletter delivered in weekly dispatches via email every Sunday.

The challenge

Conceived as a weekly dispatch infused with breezy, sardonic cosmopolitanism, Air Mail's email-only format was reaching its limits.

Old site

With few options for variety or curation, users didn't have a enough incentive to scroll or subscribe. The content was great, we just needed to advocate it.

The solution

The new, rebranded Air Mail added a homepage, navigation, and archive IA, creating a fresh experience still grounded in the weekly issue cadence.

The new Air Mail was a surprise feature in the Apple 2021 Keynote! I guess Tim Cook is really into British royal gossip?

Big bold headline that denotes a new section

Content strategy

If you don't have your story straight, no one will read it. For Air Mail's redesign, we started with the content and built up from there.

Old UX

Email, issue, and a reverse-chron archive. Simple, but restrictive. Users were given the latest issue but had to hunt and peck to find more content.

New UX

Adding a homepage and archive network provided multiple points of entry without breaking the site format or SEO.

Navigation + IA

To tie the new UX together, we introduced multiple contextual nav elements that promoted organic user journeys to, from, and across issues.
